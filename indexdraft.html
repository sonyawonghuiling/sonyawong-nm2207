HOW MUCH SPACE IS THERE LEFT IN SINGAPORE?

ABOUT

According to World Data.info, the population of Singapore had grown from 1.65 million in 1960 to 5.45 million in 2021. As one of the smallest countries in the world with a land area of 728km2, we are also one of the most densely populated countries with approximately 8000 people per square kilometre. Not only that, but Singapore has an accelerating growth rate in its population over the last 40 years. With this overarching threat of overcrowding and overpopulation, the Singapore government still encourages a high influx of immigrants. This makes me wonder whether Singapore can continue to accommodate such a growth in the future as land, housing, jobs - space - is scarce.

This project will be a data story on the spatial availability in Singapore. As a young undergraduate concerned about my place in Singpaore in the future, I believe that many of my peers carry the same concerns as I do. Therefore, I hope that this data story will be able to give a fair glimpse into an illustration of current statistics and make logical predictions about what may come in the near future.
